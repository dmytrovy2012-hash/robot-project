History of Robots

The history of robots has its origins in the ancient world. During the Industrial Revolution, humans developed the structural engineering capability to control electricity so that machines could be powered with small motors. In the early 20th century, the notion of a humanoid machine was developed.

Interesting Facts about Robots

From the history of origin to modern technological achievements — here is what is worth knowing about robots:

History & Origins
  • The word "robot" First used by Karel Čapek in 1920. From the Czech word robota (forced labor).
  • First industrial robots Appeared in the 1960s at General Motors factories.
  • Leonardo da Vinci As early as 1495, he created sketches of a mechanical knight.
Modern Technologies
  • Citizenship The robot Sophia became the first robot citizen (Saudi Arabia, 2017).
  • Atlas by Boston Dynamics The most advanced humanoid, capable of doing backflips and dancing.
  • Market leader Japan is the world's leading exporter of industrial robots.
Variety of Forms
  • Rescue robots Snake-like mechanisms for searching for people under debris.
  • Robot dog Spot Helps with patrols and has even worked in the Chernobyl zone.
  • Nanorobots Microscopic machines for delivering medicine inside the human body.
Ethics & Emotions
  • Facial expressions Modern robots can reproduce over 60 facial emotions.
  • Asimov's Three Laws Ethical rules that prohibit robots from harming humans.
  • AI-powered robots Digitally programmed robots with AI have been built since the 2000s.
